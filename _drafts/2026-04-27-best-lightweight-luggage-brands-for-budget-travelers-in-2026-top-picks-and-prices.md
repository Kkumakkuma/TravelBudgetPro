## The Best Lightweight Luggage Brands of 2026

### 1. Travelpro: The Trusted Name

#### Overview

Travelpro has built a solid reputation over the years, primarily known as the go-to choice for frequent travelers. The brand is often recommended by flight crews and has a range designed specifically for durability and weight savings. Specifically, the Travelpro Maxlite 5 comes to mind, which is both lightweight and versatile.

#### Key Features

- **Weight**: Only weighs around 5.4 lbs for a carry-on.
- **Durability**: Made from polyester fabric which is water-resistant.
- **Price**: Typically ranges from $120 to $200 depending on the size.

Travelpro has proven reliable, and I appreciated how easy it was to maneuver through crowded airports with their designs. I compared it directly with Samsonite, and while Samsonite offers equally durable options, Travelpro has a price edge that makes it a better buy for budget travelers keen on quality.

### 2. Samsonite: Classic and Reliable 

#### Overview

Samsonite has been a leader in the luggage industry for decades. Their products usually balance durability with innovative designs, and the Samsonite Freeform collection stands out as a lightweight option with modern aesthetics.

#### Key Features

- **Weight**: Approximately 5.5 lbs for the 20-inch model.
- **Durability**: Constructed with a rugged polycarbonate shell.
- **Price**: Priced between $130 and $230, making it a bit on the upper end but worth the investment.

In my testing, Samsonite felt slightly more robust than Travelpro, especially when we faced some unexpected weather changes during a trip. However, the weight difference was negligible, so the choice often comes down to personal preference and style.

### 3. Delsey: French Elegance

#### Overview

When it comes to aesthetically pleasing and functional luggage, Delsey is a fantastic option. Their Helium Aero model has garnered accolades for its performance and looks. 

#### Key Features

- **Weight**: Around 7 lbs for a checked bag.
- **Durability**: Scratch-resistant polycarbonate surface.
- **Price**: Pricing typically ranges from $150 to $250.

I loved the chic design of the Delsey Helium Aero, and its spinner wheels made for smooth transport. The slight increase in weight compared to its competitors can be justified by the luxury feel it brings.

### 4. AmazonBasics: Budget Price with Value

#### Overview

For those on a strict budget, AmazonBasics luggage hits the sweet spot. Their offerings, including the AmazonBasics Hardside Spinner, can be a lifesaver for travelers wanting decent quality without breaking the bank.

#### Key Features

- **Weight**: 6.5 lbs for a carry-on size.
- **Durability**: Made from sturdy ABS plastic.
- **Price**: Generally retails for about $55 to $90.

I purchased an AmazonBasics suitcase last year for an impromptu weekend trip and was surprised by its sturdiness. Although not quite on par with Samsonite in terms of longevity, it performed admirably for short jaunts.

### 5. Osprey: Best for Adventure Travelers

#### Overview

If you lean more towards adventure travel, Osprey is a brand that excels in providing rugged, lightweight options. The Osprey Farpoint is a favorite among backpackers and travelers alike.

#### Key Features

- **Weight**: Weighs about 3 lbs.
- **Durability**: Crafted from lightweight nylon.
- **Price**: Generally retails between $300 to $400.

I used the Osprey Farpoint on a month-long backpacking trip across Southeast Asia, and its lightweight frames were fantastic for various terrains. The mesh back panel provided extra ventilation during sweaty hikes.

### 6. Briggs & Riley: The Premium Choice

#### Overview

For those who don’t mind spending a little more upfront for a long-term investment, Briggs & Riley features some of the best warranties. Their CX series stands out, offering unique expansion capabilities.

#### Key Features

- **Weight**: About 6.5 lbs for a carry-on.
- **Durability**: Reinforced for long-lasting use.
- **Price**: Typically going for $300 and up.

During my tests, I was impressed by how the Briggs & Riley expandable options saved me from having to check a bag on returning from a long trip. Plus, their lifetime warranty makes buying this brand a no-brainer for long-term travellers.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### "All Lightweight Luggage is Expensive"

Many believe that lightweight luggage synonymous with higher cost is undeniable. However, brands like AmazonBasics prove that affordable options can deliver on lightweight promises. 

**Why this matters:** When I tried an AmazonBasics bag, I found it surprisingly sturdy relative to its price, offering an accessible option for budget travelers.

### "You Only Need a Carry-on"

While carry-ons can offer convenience, they often have limited packing space. I learned this the hard way on a multi-week trip; I ran out of clean clothes by the fourth day. 

**Why this matters:** Understanding that packing versatility can save you both money on excess baggage fees and hassle on longer trips can change how you approach travel.

### "All Brands Are Similar"

While many brands may appear to offer similar features, their construction quality and long-term durability vary significantly. Not all nylon fabrics are created equal.

**Why this matters:** After testing various brands, I found that even a seemingly minor factor like zippers could make or break your experience. Travelpro, for example, uses high-quality YKK zippers that enhance longevity over inferior options.

## Is It Worth It?

Choosing lightweight luggage will vary depending on your travel habits. If you're a frequent traveler or planning a long journey, investing in a durable brand like Travelpro or Briggs & Riley is worth every penny. However, for occasional trips, the AmazonBasics might be ideal. 

Ultimately, lightweight luggage can enhance your travel experience by minimizing the hassle at airports and on the road.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should I expect to spend on lightweight luggage?

Most quality lightweight luggage options range from $50 to $400, depending on features and brands. Budget-conscious travelers should consider options like AmazonBasics, while frequent flyers might prefer brands like Samsonite.

### What length should I consider for carry-on luggage?

A standard carry-on should be within 22 inches tall to comply with most airlines. I’ve found that any larger can often lead to unexpected fees at check-in.

### How long until I see results from better luggage choices?

You’ll likely notice the difference immediately in terms of convenience and comfort during travel. After using lightweight options, it was clear to me just how much easier it made bouncing from terminal to terminal.

### What should I look for in a durable suitcase?

Look for features like reinforced corners, high-quality zippers, and water-resistant materials. Brands like Delsey offer excellent durability while maintaining a lightweight design.

### Are there alternatives to traditional rolling luggage?

Absolutely! Backpacks like the Osprey Farpoint offer fantastic flexibility for adventure travel. They are often lighter and can be carried over rough terrain more easily than a traditional suitcase.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Lightweight Luggage

### What Size Is Considered Lightweight Luggage?

Lightweight luggage typically refers to bags that weigh under 7 lbs in their empty state. For carry-ons, the standard dimensions hover around 22 x 14 x 9 inches. Most airlines allow this size for free, maximizing convenience for budget travelers like me.

### Can I Take Lightweight Luggage on Budget Airlines?

Absolutely! Many budget airlines allow passengers to bring lightweight carry-ons without extra fees. I took my Travelpro on several low-cost carriers within Europe and appreciated how hassle-free the boarding process was.
