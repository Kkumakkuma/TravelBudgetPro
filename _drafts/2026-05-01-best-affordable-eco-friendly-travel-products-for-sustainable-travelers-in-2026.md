## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### Only focusing on popular brands is insufficient.
These guides often highlight big brands without considering smaller, high-quality alternatives. Why this matters: While brands like Hydro Flask are fantastic, lesser-known options can perform just as well, often at a lower price point. I discovered solid alternatives with great sustainability credentials that cost upwards of $10 less.

### Eco-friendly products are always expensive.
Many believe sustainable travel products come at a premium, misplacing the value. Why this matters: The cost of eco-products often averages out when factoring in their lifespans and multi-uses. For instance, Ethique's shampoo bar costs $15 but replaces up to three plastic bottles of shampoo — it’s a long-term savings win.

### Not all biodegradable products degrade efficiently.
Many assume all biodegradable options will break down properly. Why this matters: Biodegradable products often need specific conditions to decompose. From my experience with Pela phone cases, proper disposal practices can make a significant difference in reducing environmental impact.
