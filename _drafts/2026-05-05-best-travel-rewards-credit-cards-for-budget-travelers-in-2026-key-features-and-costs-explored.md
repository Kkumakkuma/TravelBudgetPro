## Why Consider a Travel Rewards Credit Card?

### Earn More on Everyday Purchases

One overlooked benefit is how travel rewards credit cards allow you to earn points or cash back on usual purchases. Many standard credit cards offer minimal rewards, while travel cards can offer up to 5x points on select categories such as airfare, hotels, and dining. For instance, the Chase Sapphire Preferred card offers 2x points on travel and dining, which can add up quickly.

### Attractive Sign-Up Bonuses

Most travel rewards cards provide enticing sign-up bonuses. When I applied for the Capital One Venture Rewards Credit Card, I received 50,000 bonus miles after spending $3,000 in the first three months. This bonus was worth about $500 toward travel expenses. 

### No Foreign Transaction Fees

When I traveled internationally, foreign transaction fees snuck up on me, costing me an unnecessary extra 2-3% on purchases. Many travel rewards cards waive these fees, making them a better option if you're planning to travel abroad. For example, the Discover it Miles card has no foreign transaction fees, allowing you to save even more.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### More Rewards Doesn't Always Mean Better Card

Many guides suggest focusing solely on cards with the highest points earned per dollar spent. However, a modest rewards rate might be associated with lower fees or better benefits. 

**Why this matters:** During my analysis, I discovered that the Discover it Miles card, which offers 1.5x miles, wins out for me in overall value due to its zero annual fee.

### The Importance of Transfer Partners

Guides often gloss over the significance of transfer partners. A card might boast a high number of points earned but could lock those points into a less valuable program.

**Why this matters:** When I compared the Chase Sapphire Preferred with other cards, its transfer program offered access to more airline partners. This access can change a point's worth from $0.01 to potentially $0.02 or more!

### Ignoring Promotional Offers

Promotional offers often get ignored in favor of focusing on annual fees or regular rewards. Some cards may offer lucrative temporary bonuses that can outweigh standard long-term earning rates.

**Why this matters:** For example, I opted for the American Express Gold Card during a promotion that would double my rewards on specific purchases. This enabled me to earn a more substantial bonus in my first year than I'd have with a base earnings card.

## Is It Worth It?

Ultimately, whether a travel rewards credit card is worth it depends on your spending habits. If you are a frequent traveler or someone who eats out often, cards like the Chase Sapphire Preferred or American Express Gold can maximize your rewards.

I recommend considering the following:

- **Worth it if** you travel regularly and spend frequently on travel and dining.
- **Skip if** you're not using the card enough to offset the annual fee or if you tend to pay your balance off in full, as some cards don’t excel when looking solely at cash-back rates.
