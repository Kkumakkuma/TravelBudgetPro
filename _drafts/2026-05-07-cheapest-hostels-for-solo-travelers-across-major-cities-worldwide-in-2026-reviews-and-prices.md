## What Most Guides Get Wrong
You'd think choosing the cheapest hostel guarantees the best value, but that isn’t always the case. 

### 1. Location Isn't Everything
Contrary to popular belief, hostels that seem central often come with noise and crowding. 
**Why this matters:** For my trip to Paris, I chose a quieter hostel a metro ride away to avoid high prices. It made for better sleep, which I realized was crucial for long days of exploration. 

### 2. Facilities Matter More Than Price
Many travelers overlook the importance of shared facilities. 
**Why this matters:** At Chill Hostel in Bangkok, I found a mini-library and a quiet reading corner. The amount I saved by dining in their kitchen turned my trip from frugal to fascinating. 

### 3. Free Events Make a Difference
A great view isn’t the only essential factor; free events can elevate your experience. 
**Why this matters:** In Berlin, I learned that attending the hostel’s guided tours significantly enhanced my understanding of the city. I’d have missed out on fantastic stories and tips had I opted just to roam solo.
