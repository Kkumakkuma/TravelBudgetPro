## How Can I Book Last-Minute Flights Under $200?

Booking last-minute flights under $200 is quite feasible, and here’s how it works. By monitoring prices, employing flexible travel dates, and leveraging specific apps, you can access unbelievable deals. The key is to be swift and adaptable.

### 1. Use Flight Comparison Websites

Websites like Skyscanner and Google Flights allow you to compare prices from various airlines in real-time. When I planned my Tokyo trip, Skyscanner helped me spot that $180 ticket quickly. To maximize savings, set price alerts on these platforms to get notifications on fare drops.

### 2. Be Flexible with Your Dates

Being flexible with flight dates can make a huge difference. For instance, flying mid-week or at the last minute can reveal significant discounts. I revised my itinerary a couple of times on a previous trip, which saved me over $50 compared to weekend flights.

### 3. Explore Nearby Airports

Sometimes, flying out of a slightly more distant airport can save you a considerable sum. While researching my last-minute options for a weekend getaway, I found that choosing a secondary airport a few miles away from Seoul saved me $30 on a ticket.

## What’s the Best Time to Book Last-Minute Flights?

The ideal time to snag a great last-minute deal generally falls within a 7-14 day window before departure. Airlines often lower prices to fill seats nearing the flight date, especially for mid-week flights. 

### Booking Trends

- **Tuesdays and Wednesdays:** These mid-week days often host the most discounted fees as airlines adjust their prices.
- **Thursdays and Fridays:** These tend to have higher fares due to increased demand as weekend travelers begin booking.

Using this knowledge, I managed to find a round-trip flight from Seoul to Bangkok for just $190 last October by booking on a Tuesday.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### Last-Minute Tickets Are Always More Expensive

Many travelers believe last-minute tickets are universally high-priced, which simply isn't true. "Why this matters:" Understanding that airlines often lower prices last minute allows you to avoid panic-buying tickets that could be much cheaper.

For instance, on my Bali trip in January 2023, I found a ticket for just $170 when many assumed the pricing was locked in at higher fares weeks ahead.

### Apps Aren't Necessarily the Best Option

While mobile apps can be convenient, not all offer the best deals. "Why this matters:" Budget travelers need to know that some apps charge fees or have limited flight options compared to desktop versions.

After a frustrating experience with an app that quoted me a higher price than the desktop site, I learned to double-check the same routes online for potential savings of $40-$50.

### Loyalty Programs Don’t Always Apply 

Many believe that loyalty programs guarantee lower fares. "Why this matters:" Airlines often reserve competitive prices for new customers to attract business, meaning loyalty doesn't always equal savings.

On a recent round-trip flight with Delta, I discovered, to my surprise, that new users could score the same route for $75 less than based on my loyalty status.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long before departure should I book my last-minute flight?
Booking 7-14 days prior to departure is usually the sweet spot to find affordable last-minute tickets. This timeframe allows airlines to adjust prices based on demand, leading to potential savings.

### What are the best days to find cheap flights?
Mid-week, especially Tuesdays and Wednesdays, often presents the best opportunities for cheaper flights. Many airlines release fare discounts early in the week, making it easier to snag deals.
