## Why You Need a Portable Wi-Fi Device

### The Freedom of Connectivity
In today's digital age, being connected is paramount. As a digital nomad, you don't want to rely solely on local internet connections, which can be unpredictable. A portable Wi-Fi device not only saves time but can also help you avoid additional costs associated with unstable networks.

### Increased Productivity
Working remotely requires uninterrupted connections. My experience has shown that having my portable Wi-Fi device boosted my productivity significantly. Instead of wondering if that café Wi-Fi would allow large file uploads or video calls, I could work confidently without interruptions.

## Features to Look For in Portable Wi-Fi Devices

### Speed and Performance
Wi-Fi device speed is usually measured in Mbps (megabits per second). Look for devices that offer at least 4G LTE speeds, which can comfortably support video calls and large downloads.

### Battery Life
When you're out exploring, you don’t want your device to die unexpectedly. Look for a portable Wi-Fi device that offers at least 12 hours of battery life on a single charge. 

### Global Coverage
If you're traveling across different countries, global coverage is essential. Devices that support multiple networks, such as Europe, Asia, and North America, will help you stay connected as you wander.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### 1. The Importance of Data Coverage
Many guides downplay the importance of data coverage in favor of device specs. From my personal experience, the coverage you receive can vary significantly from country to country. Ensure the device you choose offers coverage in all your planned destinations.

### 2. Battery Life Underestimated
Another common mistake is underestimating actual battery life. Real-world usage often yields shorter battery life than advertised. During trips where I used my Wi-Fi for heavy downloading or video calls, I noticed significant battery drain. Opt for devices that exceed your expected needs.

### 3. The Need for Local SIM Cards
Some guides suggest users rely solely on portable Wi-Fi devices without considering local SIM cards. While I often prefer devices, I’ve found that having a local SIM can be cheaper and more reliable when staying long-term in one place.
