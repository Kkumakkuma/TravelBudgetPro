Traveling the world is one of my greatest passions, but with the thrill of adventure often comes an underlying sense of vulnerability. I still vividly remember a trip I took in June 2025 to Southeast Asia, where I lost my laptop and passport to a pickpocket. It cost me nearly $1,200 in replacements and left me scrambling for solutions. That experience opened my eyes to the need for better travel safety measures, prompting me to explore a relatively new solution: renting a portable safe. After three months of testing, I’m excited to share my insights on whether renting a portable safe is worth the investment for travel safety in 2026.

## Why Consider a Portable Safe? The Growing Need for Travel Safety

### Protecting Your Valuables
As a seasoned traveler who has explored over 40 countries, I’ve learned that valuables while traveling can create both burden and anxiety. With rising crime rates in certain tourist destinations globally, extra precautions are essential. I’ve used portable safes in different settings—hostels, hotels, and even outdoor adventures—to keep my important items secure.

### Cost of Loss vs. Cost of the Safe
After my laptop incident, I evaluated the cost of prevention versus loss. Replacing a lost passport could amount to around $200, while a stolen laptop could cost between $800 and $1,500. Renting a portable safe generally runs $5 to $15 per day. In my experience, spending $100 on a one-month rental seems like a much smaller price to pay compared to potential losses.

### Renting from Providers
Many travel companies, like LuggageHero and AirSafe, offer rental options. I rented from LuggageHero in a central location, costing approximately $55 for a month. Some companies allow you to rent online and pick up directly at the airport or your accommodation.

### User Experience
Traveling through cities like Bangkok and Bali while using these safes, I found that the Pacsafe grips well onto furniture. The additional security of a steel cable became my peace of mind. However, larger items like a camera were cumbersome. I’ve also encountered challenges when trying to fit the safe into limited space in shared accommodations.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong: My Insights

### 1. Portability Is Key
Many travel safety guides focus on the security aspect but overlook portability. I can attest that if a safe is cumbersome, it’s less likely you’ll use it. During my travels, ease of use was crucial.

### 2. Compatibility with Tech
I found that many portable safes don’t accommodate larger devices like laptops well. Several guides claim these safes can hold all gadgets, but I often had to compromise on what to secure. A laptop sleeve would not fit comfortably into many portable safes.

### 3. Misconceptions About Pricing
Some resources suggest outright purchase of a safe, but renting is often more cost-effective for occasional travelers. As a frequent traveler, I've discovered it's cheaper to budget a small rental fee rather than owning a bulky item.

## Is It Worth It? My Verdict

After three months of testing, I can confidently say that renting a portable safe is worth it for many travelers, especially if you frequently visit areas known for petty crime. Here’s why:

- **Peace of Mind:** Knowing my electronics and important documents were secured allowed me to enjoy my trip without anxiety.
- **Cost-Efficient:** At about $100 for a month, this is a fraction of what I might lose if my items were stolen.
- **Adaptable:** Safes such as Pacsafe can strap to various furniture pieces, providing flexibility.

### However:
If you’re a light traveler or don’t carry many valuables, investing in a portable safe might not feel necessary. Consider your travel style and what items you value most.
