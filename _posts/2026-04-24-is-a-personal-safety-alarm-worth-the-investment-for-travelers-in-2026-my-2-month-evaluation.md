## Understanding Personal Safety Alarms

### What Are Personal Safety Alarms?
Personal safety alarms are compact devices designed to emit a loud sound in case of an emergency. Ranging from beeping to continuous tones, these alarms can draw attention and potentially deter attackers. As I traveled through bustling streets in locations such as Bangkok and Bali, the ability to alert those around me provided an extra layer of comfort.

### How They Work
When you activate a safety alarm—often by pulling a pin or pressing a button—it emits a sound that can range from 100 to 130 decibels. Just to give you an idea, that’s louder than a chainsaw! This can help signal for help or scare off potential threats.

### Why You Might Need One
- **Solo Travel**: If you often find yourself traveling alone, a safety alarm can be a reliable companion.
- **High-Crime Areas**: Many urban destinations have neighborhoods where crime is prevalent; an alarm can help draw attention in risky situations.
- **Public Transport**: I found that crowded places, especially buses and trains, can be prime spots for pickpockets. Having an alarm handy proved useful for peace of mind.
