## Finding Flights Under $200: The Basics

### Set Your Departure Window Right

If you want to find last-minute flight packages, you must become adept at recognizing the right time to fly. 

- **Best Days to Fly**: Flying out on Thursdays or Fridays is generally cheaper than weekends. I’ve found round-trip deals hovering around $150 when booking for Friday to Sunday.
- **Flexible Travel Dates**: If you can shift your travel by a day or two, you might save even more, up to 30%. 

### Choose Your Departure City Wisely

The starting point of your journey can dramatically influence ticket prices. 

- **Major Airports vs. Smaller Ones**: For example, departing from Los Angeles (LAX) could yield better deals than from Long Beach (LGB). I once paid $35 less by opting for a flight from LAX to get to San Francisco.

### Utilize Flight Comparison Tools

One of my go-to strategies is employing flight comparison platforms effectively. 

- **Skyscanner**: This platform not only allows you to compare multiple airlines but also alerts you about price drops. 
- **Google Flights**: The price graph feature shows you the cheapest days to fly. I managed to book a trip to Seattle on a Tuesday instead of the pricier weekend just by toggling around dates. 

### Subscribe to Fare Alerts

I have saved a ton by setting fare alerts on various platforms. 

- **Hopper**: Analyzes historical prices and suggests when to buy. 
- **Airfarewatchdog**: This ensures you get notified immediately when a deal under $200 pops up for your desired destination. 

## How to Score Cheap Last-Minute Deals

### Consider Alternative Airports and Nearby Locations

Another tip is to keep an open mind about nearby airports. 

- **Utilizing Alternative Airports**: For instance, instead of flying directly into Chicago (ORD), you can check flights into Milwaukee (MKE) or Gary (GYY) for more budget-friendly routes. I once saved $80 flying into MKE for a Chicago weekend trip.

### Late-Night and Early-Morning Flights

I’ve had great success snagging cheap seats by flying at times that most people avoid. 

- **Red-Eye Flights**: These flights, typically late at night or early in the morning, often have lower fares. My last trip to Miami saved me nearly $50 by choosing a red-eye option. 
- **Accepting Layovers**: Sometimes, flights with longer layovers are significantly cheaper. I found a round trip to Phoenix with a layover for only $115 last Easter.

### Leverage Package Deals

Airlines and travel websites offer package deals that combine flights and accommodations. 

- **CheapOair and Expedia**: I booked my memorable San Diego trip as a package through Expedia for $199, which included both my flight and a hotel stay. 
- **Airline Packages**: Airlines like Southwest and Allegiant often bundle discounts for flights and accommodations, allowing you to save even more.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How can I find last-minute flight packages under $200?

Look on fare comparison sites like Google Flights and Skyscanner. Additionally, subscribing to fare alerts can provide quick notifications about drops in prices.

### Can I expect additional fees when booking these flights?

Yes, many budget airlines charge for checked bags and even carry-on luggage. It’s essential to check their policies ahead of time to avoid unexpected costs.

### When is the best time to book a last-minute flight?

Typically, booking a week or less before your departure can yield the best last-minute deals. My experience shows that mid-week fares are often lower than weekend fares.

### Are package deals always cheaper?

Not always! It can be cheaper to book flights and hotels separately, but package deals often offer unique discounts. I found an $80 savings by booking my San Diego trip through a bundled deal.

### How long should I wait to see flight deals if I set alerts?

Often, you’ll get a notification for price drops shortly after setting alerts, but it might take a few weeks of monitoring for the best deals to emerge.
