## What Are Packing Cubes and How Do They Work?

Packing cubes are lightweight fabric containers designed to organize your clothes and accessories in your suitcase. They come in various sizes and shapes, allowing you to separate clothing types, like shirts, pants, or undergarments.

- **Organizational Benefits**: Packing cubes can keep similar items together. I often color-code mine: blue for tops, green for bottoms, and red for accessories.
- **Space Efficiency**: These cubes enable you to pack more effectively, often helping you save up to 30% of space in your luggage.
- **Easy Access**: You can effortlessly retrieve items without rummaging through your whole bag.

## What’s the Difference Between Packing Cubes and Compression Bags?

While packing cubes and compression bags aim to organize and save space, their functionalities differ significantly. 

- **Packing Cubes**: Best for organization, easy retrieval, and maintenance of shape. I prefer these for structured packing, as they don’t crush delicate fabrics.
- **Compression Bags**: Focused primarily on reducing volume by sucking air out. While they can save space, they often lead to wrinkled clothes.

In my experience, I recommend packing cubes for easier access during travel. On the other hand, I only use compression bags for long-term storage.

One packing technique I’ve found incredibly beneficial is the layering method. By folding items into outfits and using packing cubes for each outfit, I minimize the effort required to get dressed on my trips. For example, during a recent trip to Madrid, I used four packing cubes, one for each day’s outfits, which included tops, bottoms, and accessories.

After multiple trips with my packing cubes, I learned the importance of keeping them clean. I have dealt with funky odors or stains from traveling through humid regions. My solution has been to hand wash my packing cubes or toss them in the washing machine with a gentle detergent.
