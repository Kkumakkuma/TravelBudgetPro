## [How Much Do](/how-much-do-culinary-classes-abroad-cost-in-2026-my-real-expenses-and-insights/) Food Truck Meals Cost in 2023?

Food truck meal prices vary dramatically based on location, cuisine, and even the time of day you visit. Generally, food truck meals in major U.S. cities can be classified into three price brackets:

- **Budget meals**: $8 - $12
- **Mid-range meals**: $13 - $18
- **Gourmet meals**: $19 - $25

In cities like Los Angeles and Austin, I primarily found cheaper options between $8 and $12, while locations like San Francisco and New York dominated the gourmet scene with prices up to $25.

### Specific City Prices

- **Los Angeles**: Meals averaged around $10. Popular spots like the World Famous Kogi BBQ truck serve up Korean tacos, drawing lines of hungry patrons willing to shell out for that unique flavor combo.
  
- **San Francisco**: Expect to spend about $15 on a food truck meal. I tried a crab cake sandwich from the Golden Coast Seafood truck for $18, which blew me away but also inflated my daily expenses.

- **Chicago**: Meals ranged from $8 to $20. The Lobster Roll truck features fresh lobster rolls averaging $17, pairing beautifully with their tangy butter sauce.

## What's the Difference Between Food Trucks and Restaurants?

Navigating the differences between food trucks and traditional restaurants can be challenging yet rewarding. Here’s a quick comparison:

| Criterion                 | Food Trucks                                                  | Restaurants                                              |

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

**1. Not factoring in regional pricing differences.**  
Most guides offer a flat average for costs, which isn't necessarily useful for travelers. Prices can differ significantly from city to city.  
*Why this matters*: During my explorations, I discovered I could spend $10 on tacos in Los Angeles but would face a $15 price tag for the same class at San Francisco vendors.

**2. Solely focusing on meals without mentioning portion sizes.**  
Many misjudge food truck meals as all being cheap, failing to consider portion sizes. This can skew value assessments.  
*Why this matters*: A $12 burrito may seem like a deal, but if it’s tiny, you may be better spending $15 on something filling elsewhere.

**3. Ignoring local specialties.**  
Tourist-focused articles often overlook local favorites, leading travelers to miss unique offerings.  
*Why this matters:* I was drawn to a unique duck confit taco in Austin. A less discerning guide may have simply recommended more generic options, costing travelers on their flavor experience.

## The Hidden Costs of Food Trucks: Tips to Save Money

### Planning Your Food Truck Stops

During my food truck adventure, I quickly learned that planning my stops could significantly impact my overall costs. For example, I made the mistake of eating at a highly-rated taco truck in Seattle on a Saturday evening. The lines were long, and the prices surged to about $15 for a basic taco combo. Later that week, I visited the same truck on a Tuesday afternoon, where I snagged the same combo for only $10 and without the long wait. 

It's crucial to consider the timing when planning your food truck visits. Weekends often bring larger crowds that can drive up prices (due to limited supply) and force longer wait times. If you're flexible, I highly recommend hitting up these popular spots during off-peak hours.

### Utilizing Social Media for Discounts

Throughout my journey, I discovered how savvy food truck owners use social media to roll out promotions and discounts. For instance, I stumbled upon a Twitter post from "The Grilled Cheese Guy" in Los Angeles advertising a “Buy One, Get One Free” on Wednesdays, which helped me save a significant amount. That day, I split a $12 gourmet grilled cheese sandwich with a fellow traveler, bringing my expenses down to just $6. 

Many food trucks now announce special deals or rotate menu items that might not be regularly available, so it pays to follow them online. Facebook and Instagram were my go-to platforms, as I found tons of local food trucks posting mouth-watering pictures alongside promo codes or day-specific deals that could help avoid a pricey lunch.

## Unearthing Local Favorites: A Win for Taste and Value

### Discovering Underrated Gems

During my exploration, one surprising find was the “Wanda’s Bowl” truck in Austin, which served hearty rice and protein bowls for $10. Known less to tourists but beloved by locals, the truck was parked near the University of Texas during lunch hours.
